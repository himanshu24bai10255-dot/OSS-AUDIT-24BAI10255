  I believe in the power of open source because every day I rely
  on Git — a tool that someone built and shared freely with the
  world, asking nothing in return. That act of sharing is not just
  generosity; it is a philosophy. To me, freedom means Innovation —
  and that is exactly what open source software gives every person
  who uses it: the freedom to run, study, modify, and redistribute.

  The open source movement teaches us that knowledge grows when it
  is shared, not hoarded. When Linus Torvalds released the Linux
  kernel, or when Git was made open, they did not lose anything —
  the world gained everything. This is why I commit to carrying
  that spirit forward. One day, I intend to build Educational Resources and
  release it openly, so that someone I will never meet can stand
  on my shoulders, just as I have stood on the shoulders of those
  who came before me.

  Open source is not just a licensing model. It is a statement
  about how we believe knowledge and tools should work in the world:
  openly, collaboratively, and for the benefit of everyone.
